SQIP - pronounced /skwɪb/ - Scalable Quality Image Placeholder

The "Why":

  • Gives users a faster experience by laying out the site with content resembling the final shapes and colors of images
  • Inlined SVG does not require base64 encoding to save the HTTP request of an LQIP image
  • The SVG is significantly smaller in byte size than a comparable LQIP image (1.6kb SVG vs. 7.0kb LQIP JPEG @q15 + full smoothing)
  • Up for discussion: unlike with LQIP, users will know the SQIP is a placeholder and won't be irritated by an LQIP image's high artifact count
  • Google is using single color boxes with the most dominant color present in an image to lay out the page before images are loaded. With SQIP, we can enhance this by making the placeholder resemble both dominant shapes and colors before the high resolution raster image replaces it.
